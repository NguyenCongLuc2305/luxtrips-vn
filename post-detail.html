A Culinary Adventure across 03 regions OF VIETNAM

DAY 1

HANOI GET SETTLED

Welcome to Vietnam! Please note: Your travel documents contain a visa-on-Arrival letter (unless otherwise arranged). Please ensure to pack this in your carry-on luggage. Your Visa-on-Arrival letter must be presented to customs upon entry into the country.

Upon arrival to Hanoi's Noi Bai International Airport you will be welcomed by a hostess holding a sign with your name on it. The hostess will guide you through fast-track security services with your pre-approved E-Visa before escorting you to collect your luggage and then onwards to your awaiting guide and vehicle.

Meet your private driver and English-speaking guide for an airport transfer.

DAY 2

HANOI CULTURAL IMMERSION

Today's tour focuses on the Vietnam War, a tumultuous 20-year period spanning the history of Hanoi in Vietnam, one of Indochina's greatest American colonies, on the "Tunnel Villas." Land. Enjoy a knowledgeable local guide and joined by Mr. Chuck Searcy, a former U.S. Army member turned Vietnam Veteran's Memorial Fund project manager. This guided tour offers a unique perspective of the desperate wartime period. Explore the remaining fragments of the prison where he served as both a French colonial-era penitentiary and a symbol of resilience during the Vietnam War. Gain insights into the past while supporting efforts to heal the wounds of conflict in the region. Delve into the heart of Project RENEW, an organization dedicated to eradicating the peril of unexploded ordnance. Listen to firsthand accounts as Chuck shares his personal journey—a Fulbright war intellectual. With a rich history, from U.S. Army service to intelligence analysis during the war, his perspective is unparalleled.

In the footsteps of the late Anthony Bourdain on this unique culinary adventure in Hanoi. Embark on an array of tantalizing Vietnamese specialties that define this city's world-famous food scene. Delight in the soft and savory banh cuon, bathed by the iconic banh mi sandwich, renowned even in the Oxford Dictionary. Quench your thirst at a bia hoi on Ta Hien Street, savoring the bustling heart of Vietnamese beer beloved by locals and travelers alike. Next, visit a pho shop to relish the classic pho or try the unique pho tron, mixing pho noodles with chicken. Conclude your foodie evening with a street dining Hoan Kiem Lake, enjoying a delicious ice cream before being returned to your hotel at the evening's end. Experiencing the vibrant flavors of Hanoi.

DAY 3

HANOI A HEALTHY DOSE OF HISTORY

Journey through history on a half-day tour with a private guide, discovering the iconic landmarks of Hanoi. Begin at the Dinh Square, where you will admire the stunning architecture of the One Pillar Pagoda and Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum. Continue your exploration through the serene Imperial Citadel, a glimpse into Hanoi's regal past. Next, visit the Temple of Literature, a renowned historical monument showcasing traditional Vietnamese architecture. Witness remarkable ancient stilt houses, showcasing a unique architectural style, allowing for ventilation and protection from flooding in the city's historic neighborhoods. Next, admire the architecture of the Ngoc Hieu Pagoda, originally built in 1049, and known for its distinctive Vietnamese style with intricate wooden carvings as a symbol of devotion and resilience. Continue your exploration at the thousand-year-old Temple of Literature, the first university in Vietnam, originally built in 1070. It served as an exclusive institution for princes, nobility, and mandarins. Stroll through the detailed paths of Van Mieu Park, adorned with stone stelae atop tortoise backs, each inscribed with names of successful doctoral candidates. This UNESCO site offers a fascinating glimpse into the rich history and cultural significance of these iconic sites, gaining a deeper understanding of Hanoi's past, before you are transferred back to your accommodation.

Step into the theater and witness an ancient art form that traces its roots back over a thousand years to the farmers of the Red River Delta. Originally a boisterous hobby, water puppetry originated right here in Hanoi under the Ly and Tran dynasties. Immerse yourself in a mesmerizing performance that depicts scenes from daily life and legendary tales. The puppeteers skillfully maneuver the puppets on the water, accompanied by traditional music that adds depth and emotion to the storytelling. Delight in this unique form of entertainment that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Vietnam. After the show, you will be transferred back to your accommodation.

DAY 4

HANOI CITY STREETS AND TASTY TREATS

This morning, enjoy time at leisure to explore at your own pace, or relax at your hotel.

It's time for an exhilarating private guided journey through the hidden gems of Hanoi aboard a vintage Soviet-era jeep. Departing from your hotel, venture into fascinating history as you zip along through the bustling streets, making stops to learn about the country's rich culture. Immerse yourself with locals along the way and embrace the diverse and sometimes eccentric aspects of the city. Make a memorable stop at one of Hanoi's many bustling markets, from visit to a remote local market to a remote market to immerse yourself in the vibrant bustling atmosphere. Continue the adventurous 2-hour drive, making occasional stops to capture memorable photographs and interact with welcoming locals. Conclude your journey back at your starting point, capturing lasting memories with photographs of this unique experience. Throughout the tour, indulge in traditional Vietnamese dishes, from finger foods to the renowned egg coffee and other delightful traditional beverages.
